Mastering Gerunds, Infinitives, & Prepositions

1. Gerunds (Verb + -ing)

A gerund is a verb ending in -ing that functions exactly like a noun in a sentence.

  • Rule: Used after verbs like enjoy, practice, mind, avoid, finish.
• I enjoy coding my own grammar website.
• You should practice speaking English every day.

2. To-Infinitives (To + Verb)

A to-infinitive is the base form of a verb preceded by the word to.

  • Rule: Used after verbs like want, hope, decide, learn, agree, plan.
• I want to share my project with my classmates.
• She is learning to use GitHub for her homework.

3. Prepositions & Special Rules

Prepositions are connecting words like in, on, at, for, about, with.

Rule A: Preposition + Gerund

If a verb comes immediately after a preposition, it must be in the gerund (-ing) form.

Incorrect: Thank you for help me.
Correct: Thank you for helping me.

Rule B: Ending Sentences with Prepositions

It is perfectly natural, correct, and welcome to end a sentence with a preposition in modern spoken English!

• Formal/Stiff: The page for which you are looking.
• Natural/Modern: The page you are looking for.
