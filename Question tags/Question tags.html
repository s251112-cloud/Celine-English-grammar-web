Unit: Question Tags 🗣️❓

1. The Golden Rule of Question Tags

Question tags act like a mirror reflection. They always show the opposite tone of the main sentence statement:

Main Sentence Tone Question Tag Tone Example
Positive (+) Negative (-) You are coming to the party, aren't you?
Negative (-) Positive (+) You aren't angry with me, are you?

2. Choosing the Right Verb Tense

The verb inside your tag must always track the tense used in the primary statement.

A. Sentences with the Verb "To Be"

• She is an excellent student, isn't she?
• They were late for the workshop, weren't they?

B. Sentences with Auxiliary / Modal Verbs

• Marco can speak English fluently, can't he?
• We won't have homework over the weekend, will we?

C. Sentences WITHOUT a Helping Verb (Action Verbs)

If there is no helping verb, use do, does, or did:

• You like learning grammar, don't you?
• Celine writes beautiful web code, doesn't she?
• They passed the final English exam, didn't they?

3. Tricky Exceptions! 🚨

Watch out for these special structural rules that frequently appear on exams:

  • The "I am" Exception:
    Incorrect: I am late, am not I?
    Correct: I am late, aren't I?
  • Imperatives (Commands): Use "will you" to stay polite.
    • Close the door, will you?
  • Let's (Suggestions): Always pairs with "shall we".
    • Let's practice our English conversation, shall we?

4. Intonation: How to Say Them 🔊

  • Falling Intonation (Voice goes DOWN ↘️): Used when you are 100% sure of the fact and just want the other person to agree.
    "It's a beautiful day, isn't it? ↘️"
  • Rising Intonation (Voice goes UP ↗️): Used when you genuinely do not know the answer and are checking for confirmation.
    "You didn't see my iPad, did you? ↗️"
