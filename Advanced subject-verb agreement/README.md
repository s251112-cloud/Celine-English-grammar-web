# Subject-Verb Agreement: Advanced Rules 📝

This section covers tricky subject-verb agreement rules for specific words like "data" and "none," along with pronoun forms and verb variations.

---

## 📊 1. Data: Singular or Plural?
In modern English and standard writing style guides, **data** can be treated as either singular or plural depending on the context.

*   **Singular (Common/General Use):** Treated as a collective mass noun.
    *   *Example:* The data **is** showing that the kittens are healthy.
*   **Plural (Scientific/Formal Use):** Treated as individual facts (where "datum" is the singular form).
    *   *Example:* These data **are** clear; the female scientists published their results.

---

## 👁️‍🗨️ 2. None: Is or Are?
The word **none** can take a singular or plural verb depending on what it is referring to in the sentence.

*   **Singular:** Use a singular verb when "none" means "not one" or refers to a singular mass.
    *   *Example:* None of the milk **is** left for the puppy.
*   **Plural:** Use a plural verb when "none" refers to a plural group of individuals or items.
    *   *Example:* None of the girls **are** going to the park today.

---

## 👩‍🦰 3. Pronoun Forms
When writing about girls or animals(but also include boys), make sure to use the correct subject or object pronouns.

| Subject Pronoun (Does the action) | Object Pronoun (Receives the action) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **She** (The girl coded the page.) | **Her** (GitHub belongs to her.) |
| **It** (The cat chased the mouse.) | **It** (The girl fed it.) |
| **They** (The girls won the game.) | **Them** (The teacher praised them.) |

---

## 📋 4. Comprehensive Verb Table
Below is a reference guide for verb forms. A slash (`/`) is used if a verb has more than one variant or form depending on the subject context.

| Base Verb | Singular Form (She / It) | Plural Form (They) | Past Tense Form |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **To Be** | is | are | was / were |
| **To Run** | runs | run | ran |
| **To Code** | codes | code | coded |
| **To Have** | has | have | had |
